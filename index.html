Sandbox Manual & Guide

1. Theory Palette (Audition & Inject)

The palette automatically generates valid diatonic chords based on your Global Key & Scale settings at the top.

  • Audition: Click the main body of any chord button to hear how it sounds without adding it to your song.
  • Add to Timeline: Click the green + ADD bar at the bottom of a chord button to push it into your arrangement timeline.

2. Arrangement Timeline (The Sequencer)

This is where you build your progression. Every block represents a chord and has 4 adjustable parameters:

  • OCT (Octave): Shifts the entire chord up or down the keyboard.
  • TYPE (Quality): Changes the emotional feel (e.g., swapping a Major chord for a moody Minor 9th).
  • INV (Inversion): Flips the lowest note of the chord up an octave. Use this to flatten out wild jumps between chords for smoother Voice Leading.
  • DUR (Duration): Controls timing. 1m = Full Measure (4 beats). 2n = Half Note (2 beats). 4n = Quarter Note (1 beat).

3. Left Hand & Arp Generators

Located above the timeline, these tools automatically generate complex MIDI patterns based on your chord blocks.

  • Left Hand: Generates a bassline. "Root Block" plays long sustained notes. "Walking" algorithmically figures out diatonic passing notes based on the current scale.
  • Arp Mode: Breaks your block chords into fast, rhythmic 16th-note synthesizer patterns.

4. Dual-Bus Mixer & DSP

The audio engine splits your Left Hand (Bass) and Right Hand (Chords) into two separate signal paths.

  • The Bass is kept mono and clean to prevent low-end phasing and crackling.
  • The Chords are routed through the Master FX chain (Ping-Pong Delay, Hall Reverb, LPF Cutoff) for wide, lush spatial width.
  • A master Limiter acts as a brick wall at the end of the chain to guarantee zero digital distortion at high volumes.
